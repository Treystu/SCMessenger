Privacy Policy

This is the official privacy policy for the SCMessenger communication application, provided by Sovereign Communications.

The Short Version

We collect absolutely nothing.

We do not track you. We do not store your data. We do not have servers capable of intercepting, analyzing, or storing your messages. SCMessenger is a true peer-to-peer (P2P) protocol executing exclusively on your local device.

Data We "Collect"

None. Our infrastructure has zero insight into your identity, contacts, messages, network routing, or location.

How Your Data Is Handled

Everything related to your SCMessenger identity—including your cryptographic keys, message history, connection proofs, and relay routing configurations—is stored strictly on your device.

When you transmit a message over Bluetooth (BLE), Local Wi-Fi (mDNS), or the Internet via libp2p relays:

  • Your message is fully End-to-End Encrypted (E2EE) using modern cryptographic standards (X25519 & XChaCha20-Poly1305) before transmission.
  • No intermediary node, relay, or network provider (including us) can decrypt the payload or read its contents.

Device Permissions

SCMessenger requires certain device permissions purely to facilitate local peer network discovery and delivery:

  • Bluetooth: Required to locate, pair with, and transmit messages to nearby offline peers via BLE.
  • Local Network / Wi-Fi: Required to discover and connect with local peers via mDNS.
  • Location (Android only): Historically mandated by the Android operating system to scan for Bluetooth devices. We do not read or use your GPS coordinates.
  • Camera: Required only for scanning a peer's identity QR code to initiate a secure connection.
  • Internet: Required to route encrypted message packets through libp2p internet relays when peers are out of physical range.
